The statement
vanitas and veritas are nearly the same word — one swap of letters apart. the whole difference between them is a choice.

A big print pulls you across the room. A figure bowed over a sink, face blown to white and stippled into halftone, bringing up one gold blob. Vomit, or a nugget. You genuinely can’t tell. You reach the basin already in her posture — hunched, mid-purge, washing up or throwing up. The tap gives no water: a font that cannot clean you, nothing to wash in but more image.

Vanitas is a 400-year-old genre — Dutch still lifes crammed with skulls, dead flowers and soap bubbles, all murmuring the same thing. You are going to die; put down the goblet. The bubble was the favorite symbol. Homo bulla, man is a bubble: shiny, inflated, then not there. Mine are AI bubbles, and I mean it twice — the soap bubble the Dutch painted for fragility, and the speculative one a lot of us quietly fear we are standing inside.

The print up top is the one piece of me in the room: a self-portrait, hung and lit as a mirror so your face lands on mine and briefly wears the dissolve. It is my face on the front. Standing at the basin, it is also yours. I am not above the slop; I am admitting, on aluminium, that I am sometimes sloppy too.

The faucet handle is the off switch: hold it and the slop pauses, let go and it floods back. I freedive, and holding the handle is the same as holding a breath underwater — stillness on purpose, against everything in you that wants to let go. Pilate asked what truth was, then washed his hands of the answer. This asks the reverse. The veritas is the hand that stays, the eye that chose these frames from a hundred thousand. In a room of generated everything, that choice is the only ground truth left.

a sink with no water,
so you can’t wash your hands of it.

The glossy 20×24 print rises from the sink on a slim, load-bearing post — lure and mirror, so the whole work is freestanding and needs no wall.

A small bright screen sits in the basin under sealed acrylic, looping the polished feed. You reach for the tap the way you would reach to wash, and no water comes. It boots straight into the loop; a power cut just restarts it playing.

Accent colour
#D2914F — lifted from the feed’s gold, kept near the clay-orange of the labs that build these models. Vanitas gold and the tool’s signature at once; the only saturated colour in a cool monochrome.
The words
vanitas — Latin vanus, empty; futility, transience.
veritas — Latin, truth. One letter’s swap from vanitas.
homo bulla — “man is a bubble” (Varro, via Erasmus).
aner — Greek anēr, man — the figure the bubble stands in for.
Context — tiat · slop · sink
Made for tiat’s open call Epistemologies of Slop (151 Powell St, SF), a show on AI “slop.” Slop is “everything but the kitchen sink” — so the work throws the kitchen sink at it: a Pearlhaus freestanding stainless utility sink, two-day shipped from Amazon as part of the joke.
